Event Description

A devastating earthquake struck southeastern Turkey on February 6, 2024, with a magnitude of 7.8 on the Richter scale. The earthquake's epicenter was located near Kahramanmaraş, affecting multiple provinces including Gaziantep and Hatay.


The seismic event caused widespread destruction to residential buildings and critical infrastructure. Emergency response teams from national and international organizations mobilized immediately to provide search and rescue operations, medical assistance, and humanitarian aid to affected populations.


The disaster resulted in significant economic losses estimated at $34.2 billion, making it one of the costliest natural disasters in the region's history. Recovery and reconstruction efforts are ongoing with support from international partners.
